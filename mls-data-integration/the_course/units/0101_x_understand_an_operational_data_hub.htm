Exercise: Understand an Operational Data Hub

In this walkthrough exercise, you view customer examples of using MarkLogic for a data hub, and then look at the customer360 application that you will be creating a data hub for.

 

  1. In a browser, navigate to cdn.marklogic.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/06/MarkLogic-Hannover-Re-Case-Study_en_US.pdf.
  2. Skim through the Hannover Re case study, understanding that they use a MarkLogic data hub to bring in data from many, many data sources, to provide a 360° view of information related to an insurance application.
  3. In a browser, navigate to www.marklogic.com/press-releases/alm-transforms-information-provider-marklogic-database.
  4. Skim through the ALM case study, learning that their data hub integrates data from 60 different sources, to provide a customized view of content, for customers in the legal, consulting, insurance, finance, and real estate industries.
  5. In a browser, navigate to www.marklogic.com/customers/lagardere-active.
  6. Skim through the Lagardère Active case study, learning that they were able to quickly built applications using their data hub, having integrated content from many different silos.
  7. In a browser, navigate to www.marklogic.com/customers/healthcare-gov.
  8. Skim through the HealthCare.gov case study, understanding that many data sources were integrated, creating a seamless experience for the user.
  9. If desired, watch the beginning of the 4-minute MarkLogic Overview video, to hear about using MarkLogic for creating a data hub: www.youtube.com/watch?v=0Pz77dJOPCQ.
  10. On the desktop of your Data Integration course virtual machine (user is cent, password is centos), locate the mls-data-integration folder:


  11. Double-click the folder, to open it.
  12. Double-click the unit01 folder, to open it.
  13. Double-click the procycling_customer360.pdf file, to open it:


  14. See that it is a 360° customer view application for ProCycling, a large manufacturer of bicycles.
  15. Know that the purchases data comes from an accounting system, the support calls data from a customer support system, the contact data from marketing systems, and the triples from an .nt file.
  16. Close the file.
