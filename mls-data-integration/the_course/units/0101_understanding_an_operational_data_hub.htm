Understanding an Operational Data Hub

An operational data hub, commonly used to integrate data from multiple systems to get a complete view of an entity in real-time (e.g. a customer, an employee, a patient, a provider, a product, an asset, a person of interest, or any item of interest), provides a way to easily bring data in and out.

For example, imagine, for a customer, having data for a customer's online and in-store purchases in accounting systems, data about phone, chat, and email support incidences for the customer in a customer support system, corporate factsheet data about a customer stored a sales system, and loyalty card, contact information, contact preferences, and marketing history for customer contacts in one or more marketing systems. Imagine wanting to bring all of that data together, to have a 360° view of a customer.

Imagine being able to then view that collated data either through a custom real-time application you or your organization have created, or through a SQL-based application that your company uses.

HR Data Hub

Aetna needed, as a result of many acquisitions, to integrate over 140 employee-related systems. By bringing all of the data into one data hub, all of the information for a given employee is now easy to access, providing a real-time 360° view of an employee.

Police Data Hub

A UK policing organization uses MarkLogic to bring in data sources from multiple siloed systems (e.g. arrests database, stop and search database, traffic incidents, emergency calls system, child protection system, and missing persons system) to get a 360° view of persons of interest.

Reinsurance Data Hub

Hannover Re, a giant reinsurer, uses MarkLogic for a 360° view of insurance applications, having information relevant to an application from extensive data sources, all available through their MarkLogic data hub.

Trade Store Data Hub

ABN AMRO uses MarkLogic for a 360° view of trade data, bringing vast amounts of unstructured and structured trade data into one central operational trade data store. With a consistent, transparent record of every order and trade event, ABN AMRO is able to comply with internal and external reporting requirements in a fast and flexible manner.

Content Data Hub

Lagardère Active, a large French media company with magazines, radio stations, and TV channels, uses MarkLogic for a 360° view of publishing content, bringing in text, images, sound, and video from 15 different siloed databases for use in their operational data hub.

Healthcare Data Hub

HealthCare.gov, the U.S. national health plan marketplace, uses MarkLogic for their data hub, integrating a vast number of data sources (e.g. health plan provider systems, state systems, IRS, social security, veteran affairs, and Homeland Security), and handling hundreds of thousands of concurrent users, to provide millions of Americans access to health coverage.
