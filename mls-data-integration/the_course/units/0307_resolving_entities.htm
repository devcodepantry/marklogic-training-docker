Resolving Entities

There are many ways to resolve entities, for which there may be data coming in from a multitude of silos.

For example, imagine that the name of a person or company may have been spelled differently in different source systems.

Ways available for matching up the records for a person or company include:

  • Modifying the source documents, to ensure only one spelling
  • Doing 'or' queries, including each of the spellings
  • Matching by other record data, such as address or phone number
  • Using spell.doubleMetaphone(), for phonetic matching

spell.doubleMetaphone()

The JavaScript spell.doubleMetaphone(), or XQuery spell:double-metaphone(), function is great for reconciling names that were written down from hearing them.

For example, imagine a police organization that is attempting to gather all of the data about a person of interest, but where the person's name has been recorded with various spellings over the years, in documents from a wide variety of policing and social service systems such as emergency calls, a searched-but-not-arrested database, a traffic accident system, a sex offender system, jail systems, a couple social service systems, and reports in a police callout system.

Emily Jacobson
Emilie Jacobsen
Amilee Jakobsen
